The Process

This project had two main phases: a design phase, and a prototyping/building phase. Each phase had its own guiding principles. The process used in the project helped us figure out what to build and how to improve the space.

In the first design phase, we used a process to get from people to problems. One of the biggest focuses in this process was the properties of the space. The properties of the space are directly tied to the attitudes, values and actions of the students in the class. The goal of this design process was to capture the culture of the class and represent in the space. We were also trying to nudge the culture of the class in a slightly different direction.

Once we determined the problems and solutions within the space, we had to develop these solutions to ensure that they would have the desired effect. To do this, we used a prototyping cycle. We immediately started building things, to various levels of finish, and testing them with other students and professor. By understanding their reactions to our prototypes, we were able to make changes, or abandon ideas entirely. This cycle saved us from wasting time pursuing ideas that wouldn't fit into the overall vision of the space, and helped us narrow down to a few ideas that could make a big impact on the space.

The Design Template

We began our design process by first learning as much as we could about our users. We talked to Olin students to determine what people liked about the class and what they didn't. Some key things we found were: Principles of Engineering gave people the opportunity to fail, allowed you to make something for yourself, and was a black hole for things. We organized these insights and used them to find cultural values, and attitudes exisiting in the space.

The next step was to take these insights and turn them into properties of the space. To do this, we used a framework called the Design Template. We connected attitudes, which we found from the user insights, to actions that students and professors would do in the space. The actions are then connected to properties of the space. The properties, such as density, give us direction on how to craft our solutions for the space. For example, a sparse space might have more whiteboard, to encorage the users to craft the space to their needs.

Idea Generation

The next step was the generate as many ideas as possible to improve the space. After identifying 10 basic needs of the space, we came up with about 500 different ideas to solve these problems. We came up with some ideas that were crazy, and others that were extremely simple. These ideas were all sketched, to start thinking about how to implement or build the ideas.

Prototyping
